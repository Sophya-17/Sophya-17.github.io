What is sustainable architecture?

1Environmental damage to architecture.

The construction of buildings contributes significantly to environmental pollution, being responsible for 23% of air pollution, 40% of drinking water pollution, and 50% of landfill waste. Additionally, the sector consumes large amounts of natural resources. Noise and light pollution affect biodiversity, altering the natural cycles of animals such as bats, badgers, and birds. Air pollution, mainly from carbon dioxide emissions, contributes to global warming. These impacts affect air quality, water, and life in general.

The problems associated with environmental pollution caused by construction include air, water, and soil quality, as well as the inadequate generation and management of construction and demolition waste. Proposed solutions include the use of sustainable technologies, careful construction practices, efficient waste management, implementation of environmental regulations, and compliance with them. Despite challenges, the application of appropriate measures can help mitigate the negative impacts of construction on the environment.

2What is it?

Sustainable architecture is an approach to design and construction that seeks to reduce human impact on the environment and promote a more sustainable future. It is based on the use of eco-friendly materials, energy efficiency, and adaptability to the changing needs of users and the environment.

It aims to reduce the environmental impact of buildings throughout their life cycle, from planning and construction to operation and eventual demolition. The main goal of sustainable architecture is to create built environments that are resource-efficient, environmentally friendly, and socially responsible.

By adopting the principles of sustainable architecture, it is possible to contribute to sustainable development and address the challenges of global climate change and urbanization. Additionally, sustainable architecture seeks to conserve and protect existing natural and urban environments, promoting urban densification and efficient use of space.
